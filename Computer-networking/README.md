***Computer networking is the practice of interconnecting computing devices to enable data exchange and resource sharing via communication protocols and transmission media.***

**IP Address:**
*Its unique numerical lable assign to every device connected to a computer, in general term its a house number of connected devices. There are mainly two types of IP address IPv4 and IPv5. IP addresses are mainly catogerized into 4 parts by their scope and assignment method*

1.Public IP: Assigned by an Internet Service Provider (ISP) to our router, this is the address visible to the outside internet.

2.Private IP: Assigned by our local router to devices within our home or office network (e.g., 192.168.x.x), these are not directly accessible from the public internet.

3.Static IP: A permanent address manually configured or fixed by an ISP.

4.Dynamic IP: An address automatically assigned by a router (via DHCP) that can change each time we connect.

DNS(Domain Name System): It translate the human readable doman names (www.google.com) into machine readable IP address (74.125.239.35)
